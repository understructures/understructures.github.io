oberih

is a publishing and a fundraising project that stand as a platform and means of support for people affected by the war in ukraine. it is a collection of stories about oberihs [in ukr.: ‘an item that protects’],— things that accompany us and provide solace and protection in all circumstance. gathered from our friends, who are fighting, working, volunteering and directly facing consequences of the war, these stories reached us in the form of short texts, images, voice messages and online posts in the period of april–may 2022. after we transcribed, edited and compiled these stories together, we published them in a book called oberih.

oberih is printed in the edition of 1000 numbered copies and presented at lc-queisser gallery booth at liste art fair (basel, ch) on june 12, 2022. after the initial presentation, the book will be available for international purchase online. proceedings from donations and sales of the edition will be all directed towards volunteer initiatives and humanitarian organizations, which are related to our authors and currently have urgent necessities in their activity on territories affected by the war.


Initatives we support

  • Atelier Normalno
    group of artists with and without Down syndrome; right now, two artists from the group: Katya Libkind and Stas Turina are working and volunteering in various initiatives and funds that support people with disabilities.

  • Ukraine Helping Medical Collection
    a joint group of Poland and Ukrainian citizens, organising a collection of funds and specific medical and tactical products in Poland. The collected funds are used to purchase the most needed products, according to the needs of hospitals and auxiliary wards.

  • Hospitaliers
    a volunteer organization of paramedics, working directly on the front line.

  • IT-Troups
    a volunteer organization providing wide range of various humanitarian aid and media coverage of the war in Ukraine.

  • Women Veteran Movement
    a consolidation of female veterans, created to increase the opportunities for self-realization in the society and to provide advocacy and protection of the rights of women veterans and active military personnel.
